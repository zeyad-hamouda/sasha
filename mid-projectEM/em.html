Why study Bachelor Degree in Electro-Mechanical Engineering

BUiDâ€™s Bachelor Degree in(BSc.) Electro-Mechanical Engineering addresses the needs of the region in the industrial, manufacturing, airline, shipping, service and governmental sector. Graduates of this programme will be equipped with a wide set of relevant knowledge and skills preparing them for an employment market with very diverse needs. Owing to the integrated nature and interconnected dependency of components and sub-assemblies comprising electro-mechanical systems, the analysis, design and maintenance of these devices is no longer due to the simple application of either mechanical, electrical or electronic technology. Instead, combinations of the skills central to all of these disciplines are needed.

Learning Outcomes

Knowledge
  • Acquire knowledge of mathematics, statistics, and engineering-related sciences required to model and solve real life electro-mechanical engineering problems
  • Gain knowledge of electro-mechanical components and systems involving people, materials, equipment, information, and energy.
  • Acquire knowledge of the design, conduct and analysis of experiments in a scientific and systemic way in order to identify the optimum running conditions for such systems.
  • Identify, formulate and optimise operations and service systems based on objective performance engineering functions and constraints.
Skills
  • Use modern tools to analyse, improve, and optimise engineering systems.
  • Realise performance measurement of real systems by identifying feasible performance indicators and methods of assessment.

Environment Sciences and Sustainability

The module introduces the fundamental principles of sustainability including avoidance or minimisation of negative impacts on the environment; conservation and efficient use of natural resources; preservation of cultural patterns; and ecological harmony and respect for biodiversity.

Introduction to Engineering and Innovation

This module prepares students for an exciting career in engineering in general as well as giving students an understanding of the different professions and specialisations in Engineering.

Engineering Economy and Entrepreneurship

This module introduces methods of economic analysis in engineering, including time value of money, equivalence, economic measures of worth, selection rules for alternatives, income taxes and equipment depreciation, inflation, and uncertainty.

Introduction to Computers

The aim of this module is to examine computer systems and learn how they can enhance personal productivity.

Physics I

A study of university entry-level physics including the following topics: kinematics in one, two, and three -dimensions; dynamics; work; energy and conservation of momentum; rotational motion; and equilibrium of rigid bodies.
